FAQ

Common questions.

What types of grants do you write?
We specialize in foundation and community grants — the kind offered by private foundations, community foundations, and corporate giving programs. We do not currently support federal grants (Grants.gov), though that's coming.
Do you submit the application for us?
No — we deliver the complete written application, and you submit it through the funder's portal. This keeps your organization in control of the submission and any funder relationship.
How does Wellspring Grants deliver so quickly?
We combine professional grant writing frameworks with technology-assisted drafting and human quality review. Every grant is scored on 6 quality dimensions and revised until it meets our standards before you ever see it.
Will grant reviewers know this wasn't written entirely by hand?
Our process is designed to produce grants that read as specific, human, and compelling as any professionally written application. We prohibit generic language, require human story openings, and run every grant through a differentiation test to confirm it couldn't have been written for any other organization. The outcome is what matters — a competitive application you're proud to submit.
What if the draft needs changes?
We stand behind our work. If something doesn't read right or needs adjusting after you review it, reach out and we'll revise it.
Do I need to be a 501(c)(3)?
Most foundation grants require 501(c)(3) status, so we ask about this in the intake form. If your status is pending, we'll still write the application — just note it in your form and we'll handle it appropriately.
