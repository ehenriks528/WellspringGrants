## Executive Summary

Last fall, a seventh-grader in the 78745 zip code brought home a failing math grade — not because the material was beyond her reach, but because her family could not afford the $80-per-hour tutoring her classmates relied on, and no free alternative was open past 6 p.m.

In South Austin's 78745 zip code, 68% of students qualify for free or reduced lunch, yet only 41% graduate college-ready. Private tutoring costs an average of $80 per hour — a price that places consistent academic support out of reach for the low-income families we serve. Bright Futures Learning Center provides free after-school tutoring, mentorship, and college prep support to close that gap and increase high school graduation rates across South Austin.

With a grant of **$28,000** from the H-E-B Excellence in Education Foundation, we will hire two part-time tutors, expand programming from two to three evenings per week, launch a Saturday SAT prep cohort for 30 high school juniors, and equip students with 15 refurbished laptops — growing our reach from 120 to 150 students in the 2025–2026 school year.

## Organizational Background

Bright Futures Learning Center is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit based in South Austin, Texas, with a mission to provide free after-school tutoring, mentorship, and college prep support to underserved youth — closing the opportunity gap and increasing high school graduation rates in one of Austin's most under-resourced communities.

We serve middle and high school students in grades 6–12 from low-income households, primarily in the 78745 and 78748 zip codes. Today, we serve 120 students annually. Eighty-five percent qualify for free or reduced lunch, and 60% are English language learners — a student population that faces compounding barriers to academic achievement and college access.

Our growth has been steady and accountable. In 2023, we received a $10,000 seed grant from the Austin Community Foundation. Since that award, we have tripled our student enrollment — a concrete signal that our program model works and that community demand for our services is real and growing.

We have also built meaningful partnerships within the Austin community. For two consecutive years, we have participated in H-E-B's school supply drive, connecting our families directly with resources that arrive at the start of each school year. That partnership reflects our shared belief that every child in South Austin deserves a fair academic start.

Our annual operating budget is $195,000. Our staff and volunteer tutors deliver programming that is free to every student we serve. We maintain lean administrative costs, reinvesting the majority of every dollar into direct student programming. Our board of directors provides active governance oversight, and our program records are reviewed quarterly to track participant outcomes against stated goals.

## Statement of Need [WORD COUNT: 247 / 250 max]

In South Austin's 78745 zip code, the gap between what students need and what their families can access is wide, measurable, and urgent. Sixty-eight percent of students in this community qualify for free or reduced lunch. Yet only 41% graduate college-ready — a disparity that does not reflect a lack of ability, but a lack of access to consistent, affordable academic support.

H-E-B Excellence in Education Foundation has long recognized that academic achievement depends on what happens outside the classroom as well as inside it. The need in our community is not theoretical — it is present in every student who walks through our door without a device to complete homework, without a college counselor to guide an application, and without a quiet place to prepare for a test that could change the trajectory of their future.

## Project Description [WORD COUNT: 499 / 500 max]

**The Need This Project Addresses**

In South Austin's 78745 and 78748 zip codes, 68% of students qualify for free or reduced lunch, and only 41% graduate college-ready. Our current program serves 120 students across two evenings per week, but student demand — and the scope of unmet academic need — has outpaced our current capacity. With support from the H-E-B Excellence in Education Foundation, we will expand that capacity in three targeted, measurable ways.

**What We Will Do**

First, we will hire two part-time tutors at 10 hours per week each for 12 months, beginning September 2025. These hires will allow us to extend programming from two evenings per week to three, accommodating a larger cohort of students and reducing the wait list that has formed as enrollment has grown.

Second, we will launch a Saturday SAT prep cohort serving 30 high school juniors. This cohort will follow a structured curriculum with materials purchased specifically for this program. Many of these students have never had access to formal SAT preparation. An average score increase of 90 points among cohort participants is our target — a gain that, for college admissions, can open doors that would otherwise remain closed.

Third, we will purchase 15 refurbished laptops to place in students' hands for homework completion, college application work, and SAT practice outside of program hours. Currently, a significant number of our 120 students lack a device at home. We will not paraphrase that reality: students without devices cannot complete online assignments, access digital textbooks, or practice the skills we teach. Closing that gap is foundational to every other outcome we aim to achieve.

**The Logic of This Approach**

The need is documented: 68% of our students qualify for free or reduced lunch, and private tutoring at $80 per hour is inaccessible to their families. Our activities — adding tutoring hours, launching SAT prep, and distributing laptops — directly address the root causes of that need. The outputs are specific: 150 students served (a 25% increase), 30 seniors completing college applications with our support, 30 juniors receiving SAT preparation, and 15 devices placed with students who currently have none. The outcomes — an 80% on-time graduation rate among participants and a 90-point average SAT score increase — reflect what consistent, free academic support can produce. The long-term impact is a South Austin community where zip code is not a reliable predictor of college readiness or graduation.

**Timeline**

Programming will run September 2025 through August 2026. Tutor hiring will be completed by August 2025. Laptop procurement and SAT curriculum purchasing will occur in August and September 2025, prior to the program launch. Saturday SAT prep cohort sessions will begin in October 2025 and run through April 2026, aligned with spring standardized testing windows.

H-E-B Excellence in Education Foundation's commitment to expanding access to quality education in Texas communities is directly reflected in this project design. Every dollar requested will reach a student in South Austin who has no other free, structured alternative available to them.

## Goals and Objectives

**Goal 1: Increase the number of South Austin students receiving free academic support.**

- **Objective 1.1:** Serve 150 students during the 2025–2026 school year (September 2025–August 2026), a 25% increase over our current annual enrollment of 120 students, as measured by program intake records and monthly attendance logs.
- **Objective 1.2:** Extend program availability from two evenings per week to three evenings per week by September 2025, as documented by the updated program calendar and tutor scheduling records.

**Goal 2: Increase college readiness among high school participants.**

- **Objective 2.1:** Achieve an 80% on-time graduation rate among program participants in the 2025–2026 cohort, as tracked through school district graduation records and participant exit surveys collected in May and June 2026.
- **Objective 2.2:** Support 30 seniors in completing college applications with direct staff guidance, as measured by completed application verification logs maintained by our college prep coordinator.

**Goal 3: Improve SAT preparedness for high school juniors with no access to private test prep.**

- **Objective 3.1:** Achieve an average SAT score increase of 90 points among the 30 students who complete the Saturday SAT prep cohort, as measured by comparing official College Board pre-program and post-program score reports submitted by participants.

H-E-B Excellence in Education Foundation has consistently invested in programs that produce measurable academic gains for Texas students. These objectives are designed to produce exactly that — specific, documented improvements in the academic trajectories of students who have no other free alternative in South Austin.

## Evaluation Plan

Our evaluation approach is straightforward, consistent, and tied directly to each project objective.

**Data Collection and Tracking**

Program intake forms, signed at enrollment, will establish a baseline for each participant, including grade level, household income status, English language learner status, device access at home, and current academic standing. Monthly attendance logs will track student participation across all three weekly program evenings and Saturday SAT sessions.

**Outcome Measurement**

For graduation rate tracking, our college prep coordinator will cross-reference participant records with Austin Independent School District graduation data each June. For college application completion, we will maintain a verified log of each senior's submitted applications with confirmation from students and, where possible, school counselors.

For the SAT cohort, we will collect official College Board score reports from participants at program entry and following the spring 2026 test administration. Score comparisons will be calculated individually and averaged across all 30 cohort members to determine whether we have met our 90-point average increase target.

**Reporting**

A mid-year progress report will be prepared in February 2026, summarizing enrollment numbers, attendance rates, and preliminary SAT cohort data. A final program report will be completed by September 2026 and submitted to the H-E-B Excellence in Education Foundation, including all outcome data, enrollment counts, and a narrative summary of program adjustments made during the year.

Program data will be reviewed internally by our executive director on a monthly basis throughout the grant period.

## Sustainability Plan

The 15 refurbished laptops will become permanent organizational assets, available to students in subsequent program years at no additional cost. The SAT curriculum purchased in Year 1 will be reused in Year 2 with only consumable material replacement needed.

We will also develop an individual donor campaign in spring 2026 targeting South Austin community members and local businesses. Our annual operating budget of $195,000 reflects an organization that has successfully diversified its revenue base since 2023, and we are committed to maintaining that discipline.
